# Data Sources

Use public-web evidence first. The output is a market brief, not a forensic due-diligence file.

## Input Sources

- `1688.com` supplier storefront
- `Alibaba.com` company or product storefront
- company independent website
- public social profiles
- public marketplace listings

## Best Search Patterns

### Competitor discovery

- `"<supplier name>" competitor`
- `"<core product>" manufacturer`
- `"<core product>" OEM ODM`
- `site:linkedin.com/company "<core product>"`
- `site:alibaba.com "<core product>" manufacturer`
- `site:1688.com "<core product>"`
- `site:instagram.com "<brand name>"`

### ICP and buyer discovery

- `"<core product>" distributor`
- `"<core product>" hotel procurement`
- `"<core product>" project supplier`
- `"<core product>" salon`
- `"<core product>" case study`

### SEO / GEO discovery

- `site:<competitor-domain> blog`
- `site:<competitor-domain> inurl:case`
- `site:<competitor-domain> inurl:faq`
- `site:<competitor-domain> "<comparison keyword>"`

## Evidence Priorities

Prefer evidence in this order:

1. official supplier or competitor website
2. official company social profile
3. marketplace storefront
4. public directory / procurement page
5. reputable third-party coverage

Do not treat anonymous directory claims as equal to first-party evidence.

## Contact and People Data

- Use only public emails, forms, phone numbers, and public LinkedIn profiles.
- If a person cannot be verified from a real profile, say `Not publicly verified`.
- Do not guess names from email prefixes.

## What Counts as Proof

- certifications page
- downloadable catalog
- project or case-study page
- trade-show or partner page
- visible product specs
- social proof with consistent branding
- SEO footprint across indexed category and content pages

## What to Avoid

- fake traffic estimates stated as fact
- fake certification claims
- copied vendor names from AI snippets without first-party confirmation
- repeating SEO/GEO best practices without mapping them to the supplier's actual gaps
